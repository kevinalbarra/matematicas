Conference Information

The IX International Conference / Days of Applied Mathematicshas as its main purpose to share concrete experiences regarding mathematical knowledge in the resolution of problem situations seen from different contexts. In this sense, we invite the academic-scientific community (students, teachers and researchers) to present their own research work. Within the framework of the conferences, participants can engage in the conference activities in (03) three different ways: giving a lecture without submitting a publication, giving a lecture submitting publication, and attending the lectures given. In the case of the first two, you can make an oral dissertation or poster presentation.

You can participate in the IX International Conference / Days of Applied Mathematics with an oral presentation, for which you must submit an abstract of the research work. It is important that our speakers make a pre-registration in the event website including the title, abstract and keywords, both in English and Spanish. This will be reviewed in order to confirm the relevance of the article with the topics of our event which corresponds to the first participation requirement for subsequent registration in the event.

For the IX International Conference / Days of Applied Mathematics in 2022, the organizing committee will hold the conference through hybrid participation (On-site or Online), in case of On-site presentation, the costs of the mobilities should be covered by our speakers or their sponsoring institution.

Oral presentations at theIX International Conference / Days of Applied Mathematics should follow the institutional template given by the hosting institution and should have the following information: title, introduction, objectives, methodology, results, conclusions and references. The author of each research paper approved by the committee must send a pre-recorded video of the presentation on the established date according to the following indications: oral presentation (25 minutes).

All papers published in the IOP Conference Series are fully citable and, once published, will be available for free download. How to organize your paper

the IOP Conference Series uses author-supplied PDF files for all print and online publications. Authors are asked to prepare their papers using Microsoft Word o LaTeX, according to the journal guidelines and templates,and then convert these files to PDF . Full instructions on how to prepare your paper,along with templates, can be found on the IOP Conference Series website

It is important to make sure that when you submit your article,it is in final form, ready for publication and has been fully reviewed. IOP does not review papers and will not send author proofs prior to publication. Please note that post-publication changes are generally not permitted unless there are exceptional circumstances so please ensure that your paper has been checked for errors.
